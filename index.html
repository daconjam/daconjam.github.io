About Me

Hi there! I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Morgan State University as of September 2023, and the Principal Investigator of the MINDS Lab (Recruiting in Fall 2024), which conducts research in the areas of Trustworthy AI, FATE in AI, Data Science for Social Good and AI+X. I am also a Collaborator & Affiliate Faculty member of both the Center for Equitable Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Systems (CEAMLS) and the Institute for Trustworthy AI in Law and Society (TRAILS).

I earned my Ph.D. and M.S. degrees in Computer Science from Michigan State University (MSU) in 2023 and 2020, respectively, where I was supported by both an MSU UEF and NSF GRFP—all while being under the supervision of Dr. Jiliang Tang. Before that, I earned my B.S. degree in Mathematics and A.S. degree in Computer Science, from the City University of New York—Medgar Evers College in 2018.

My current research spans the areas of Trustworthy AI, Natural Language Processing, Computational Social Science, and Interdisciplinary Data Science. Specifically, he focuses on implementing empirical methods to conceptualize, examine, and mitigate socioethical implications (e.g., bias, stereotypes, and representational harms) of innovative AI technology by highlighting its negative social impacts. Moreover, I regularly serves as PC member and reviewer for multiple top-tier international conferences and journals in machine learning and data science such as ACL, ACL-IJCNLP, NAACL, AAAI, KDD, TheWebConf, and IJCAI.

Organizations

MINDS Lab

By leveraging advanced machine learning and data mining algorithms, the MINDS Lab focuses on the analysis, experimental evaluation, design and implementation of theory, logic and systems, with an emphasis on developing explainable, interpretable and theoretically sound tools to create new and innovative technology.

Webpage


ASEA Workshop

ASEA (Addressing Socioethical Effects of Artificial Intellegence) [pronounced “ay-see”] is a free and open to all socioethical workshop to address disparities in AI by conducting plenary discussions of its societal effects in full, via end-to-end cutting-edge research.
