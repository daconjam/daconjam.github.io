# Hi, I'm Jamell Dacon, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor of Computer Science and Researcher focused on Trustworthy AI for Education, Health, and Language Justice.

## About Me

I am a computer scientist focused on trustworthy AI for socially consequential domains, with research spanning education, health, language justice, and computational social science. My work develops robust, interpretable, and equitable AI systems that address bias, distribution shift, and high-stakes decision-making in real-world settings.

I am especially interested in turning technical research into practical systems that support learning, improve health outcomes, and better reflect the diversity of human language and experience.

> "Education’s purpose is to replace an empty mind with an open one." — Malcolm Forbes

## Core Expertise

- **Trustworthy NLP and Language Justice:** Research on bias, dialect variation, harmful language, and fairness in language technologies.
- **Educational AI and Learning Analytics:** AI systems for student support, pedagogical feedback, educational retrieval, and robust evaluation.
- **Health AI and Clinical Decision Support:** Personalized, data-driven methods for chronic disease management, predictive analytics, and scalable health informatics.
- **Computational Social Science and Equity Analytics:** AI and data science for studying social harms, public discourse, incarceration, and algorithmic inequity.
- **AI Robustness, Interpretability, and Distribution Shift:** Methods that improve generalization, reliability, and transparency in high-stakes AI systems.

## Research Interests

My research program centers on trustworthy AI for socially consequential domains:

- **Trustworthy AI:** Transparent, robust, explainable, and secure AI systems that prioritize accountability, privacy, and safety.
- **Trustworthy NLP and Language Justice:** Bias, dialect robustness, harmful content, and equitable language technologies.
- **Educational AI and Learning Analytics:** AI for teaching, learning, student feedback, and robust educational decision support.
- **Health AI and Clinical Decision Support:** Data-driven AI for personalized care, chronic disease management, and biomedical informatics.
- **Computational Social Science and Equity Analytics:** AI approaches that examine social harms, inequities, and societal impacts.
- **AI Robustness and Distribution Shift:** Reliable evaluation and methods that generalize across changing and high-stakes settings.
